Reasoning Levels Explained

Level Description When to Use Credit Impact
Low Fastest responses, minimal chain-of-thought Autocomplete, simple Q&A, boilerplate, quick edits Lowest
Medium Balanced reasoning, good for most tasks Standard coding, function generation, test writing, code review Moderate
High Deep reasoning with extended thinking Debugging, multi-file refactors, complex logic, architecture High
X-High Maximum reasoning depth, longest thinking time Hardest problems, novel algorithms, cross-codebase analysis Highest
⚠️ Cost Tip: Higher reasoning levels consume significantly more credits. Use Low/Medium for routine tasks and reserve High/X-High for complex problems where quality matters more than speed. Powerful models at X-High reasoning can use up to 27× the base credit cost per interaction.
💡 How to read this view: Models are grouped by their capability tier. Within each tier, they are sorted by output price (lowest first). Pick the cheapest model in a tier that meets your task complexity — you get the same category of quality at lower cost.
